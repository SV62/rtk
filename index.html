What tappers are typically paid

  • The most common arrangement in Kerala is a share system — the tapper keeps roughly a third of the latex/sheet produced (or its cash value), instead of a fixed daily wage. Exact shares are negotiated per estate.
  • Some estates instead pay a fixed daily wage regardless of yield, sometimes with a small per-kg bonus above a set quota.
  • Rates vary by estate, district, and season, and change over time — check current rates with your local Rubber Producer Society rather than relying on a fixed number here.

Good practice tips

General guidance — always follow your estate/Rubber Board extension officer's advice for your specific trees and soil.

🌅

Tap early, before the bark dries

  • Latex flows best in cool, humid pre-dawn hours.
  • Aim to finish tapping before direct sun hits the panel.
🔎 Discussions & articles
🌧️

Never tap on wet bark

  • Tapping a rain-wet panel dilutes latex and invites bark disease.
  • Wait until the panel is dry, even if it means tapping later.
🔎 Discussions & articles
🍂

Watch for monsoon disease

  • Heavy, sustained rain raises the risk of Abnormal Leaf Fall and Pink disease.
  • Consider rain-guarding panels and check for early symptoms.
🔎 Discussions & articles
🔪

Keep the tapping cut shallow

  • Cutting too deep wounds the cambium and shortens the tree's tapping life.
  • A consistent, shallow cut keeps panels productive for years.
🔎 Discussions & articles
📅

Rest days matter

  • Standard d/2 or d/3 tapping systems give the tree recovery time.
  • Skipping rest days for short-term yield wears trees out faster.
🔎 Discussions & articles
🥽

Tap safely

  • Carry a headlamp and wear closed footwear — early-morning tapping means low light.
  • Be mindful of snakes and slippery slopes.
